Software Survivor is the consulting practice of Antonio Perez, a Principal Engineer based in Fresno, California. I help established businesses and funded teams build, modernize, and connect the software their operations depend on.

The best fit is a serious business problem: a workflow that will not scale, disconnected systems that create manual work, a legacy application that has become risky to change, or a product idea that needs senior technical judgment before more money is spent.

I have spent more than a decade building production software for startups, small businesses, and enterprise environments including Target and Pressed Juicery. That background shapes the way I work: practical architecture, clear tradeoffs, maintainable implementation, and delivery plans that respect business constraints.

## How I Work

**Start with the constraint.** Before proposing a build, I want to understand the workflow, risk, users, existing systems, timeline, budget range, and what a useful first release would accomplish.

**Prefer the smallest responsible next step.** Sometimes that is a custom application. Sometimes it is an integration, an automation pilot, an architecture review, or a modernization plan that reduces risk before implementation.

**Stay close to delivery.** You work directly with a senior engineer, not a sales layer. The same person helping shape the architecture is involved in the technical decisions that determine whether the work survives production.

## Where Software Survivor Fits

- **Custom business software and SaaS products** for operations, customers, internal teams, and revenue-critical workflows
- **AI workflow automation** for document, email, review, routing, and data-entry processes
- **Systems integration** across ERPs, CRMs, e-commerce platforms, internal tools, and APIs
- **Software modernization and rescue** when an existing system is fragile, costly, or hard to change
- **Architecture and technical leadership** for founders, operators, and teams that need senior engineering judgment

## What Makes the Engagement Different

You should come away from the first conversation with more clarity than you had before it. If there is a fit, the next step is scoped around a practical outcome. If there is not a fit, I will say so and point you toward a better next step when possible.
